Economic Impacts
  • The increase in immigration has increased the earnings of Americans with more than just a high school degree in the United States.
  • Between 1990 and 2004 increased immigration was corrilated with increasing earnings of Americans by 0.7 percent. It is expected to contribute to an incease of 1.8 percent over the long term.
  • Most immigrants are also more likely to create there own jobs. According the US deparment of labor 7.5% of the foreign born are self employed.
  • Comprehansive immigration reform help support and create up to 900,000 new jobs within 3 years of the reform.
  • Immigrants boost demand for consumer goods. Latinos and Asians who are immigrants will reach 1.5 trillion and 775 billion dollars alone.
  • Immigrants are 30% more likely to start their own businesses than non-immigrants. 18% of small business owners in the US are immigrants.

Population
  • From 2010-2017 the immigrat population had grown enormously. More than 4.6 million migrated between 2010 and 2017 and 17.1 million were born with an immigrant parent
  • Recent immigrants are likely to be from Mexico, China, India, Philippines. These immigrants are also more likely to get a college degree.
  • From 2000-2017 imigrants have been migrating to Texas, California, Florida, New York, and New Jersey. Both Texas and California have almost 2 million imigrants.
  • As of 2015 there are about 47 million immigrants in the United States. This is 19.1% of 244 million international migrants worldwide and 14.4% of the US population.
  • There is approximately 1 million immigrants that migrate to the US every year legally. The number of illegal immigrants who migrate to the US is unknown.
  • Both documented and undocumented imigrants made up 16.9% of the US work force in 2016 which is 13.3 percent higher than in 2000.
